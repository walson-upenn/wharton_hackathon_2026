Amenity Taxonomy & Pruning

Raw amenity strings from the Expedia dataset are inconsistent and noisy. GPT-4o maps each raw string to a clean canonical name — or prunes it entirely if it's not guest-relevant. This ensures every downstream LLM call works with concise, meaningful labels.

Amenity Importance Ranking

Not all amenities matter equally. GPT-4o-mini reads the property description and produces a strict ordered ranking of every pruned amenity — most to least important — combining general travel knowledge with property-specific context (e.g. a spa retreat puts spa at the top; a budget hostel puts free wifi first). The ordering is then normalized to a continuous 0–1 criticality score: rank 1 → 1.0, rank last → 0.0, linear in between. This replaces the old four-tier system and eliminates hard score jumps between neighbouring amenities.

Per-Review Analysis Pipeline

Each review is processed through three stages. First, the LLM identifies which amenities are mentioned. Then sentiment and detail depth are scored in parallel on the confirmed mentions only. Finally, concrete reasons are extracted for any non-neutral ratings.

AskScore

AskScore ranks which amenities are most worth asking future reviewers about. It weighs four signals: how much detail coverage we already have, how much reviewers disagree, whether sentiment is trending down, and how stale our last mention is — all scaled by how critically important the amenity is.

AskScore = Criticality × (0.40·DetailGap + 0.20·Disagreement + 0.20·Trend + 0.15·Recency)
DetailGap = 1 − Coverage × (avg_detail / 4)  ·  Coverage = 1 − e−n/10
Disagreement = σ(sentiments) — population std dev across all mentions
Trend = max(0, avg_old_sentiment − avg_recent_sentiment) — fires only when declining
Recency = 1 − e−days_since_last_mention / 120 — stale data scores high
Criticality is the rank-normalized score from Step 2 — the top-ranked amenity gets 1.0, the bottom-ranked gets 0.0, all others are linearly spaced in between. It gates the whole score.   DetailGap (weight 0.40) blends coverage (how many reviews mention this) with depth (how concretely they described it).   Disagreement (weight 0.20) surfaces amenities where reviewers sharply disagree — high variance means the next guest's experience is unpredictable.   Trend (weight 0.20) flags amenities whose sentiment has recently declined, signalling a potential service change.   Recency (weight 0.15) penalises amenities whose last mention is stale so we refresh outdated coverage.
